Welcome to the Birthday Problem!

Problem Introduction

The birthday problem relies on a few assumptions. Assume that there are 365 days in every year (we are going to ignore leap years), and assume that the probability of being born on a given day is the same for all days of the year. Thus, with this assumption you have a 1⁄365 chance of being born on July 2nd, for example.

Using these assumptions, the birthday problem asks what is the minimum number of people that must be in a room such that it is more likely than not that two people in the room share a birthday.

The Case for Two People

If there is only 1 person in the room, the birthday problem is trivial. There is a 0% chance of two people sharing a birthday since there is only 1 person.

If there are only 2 people in the room, the problem is still simple. The probability that the 2 people share a birthday is 1⁄365 as the second person in the room has a 1 out of 365 chance of being born on the same day as the first person.

2 People

P(The 2 people share a birthday) = 1 × 1⁄365 = 1⁄365
